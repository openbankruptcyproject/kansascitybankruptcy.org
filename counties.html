Bankruptcy by County -- Kansas City Metro Area

Your county determines which federal court handles your bankruptcy case and which state exemptions protect your property. Find your county below for specific information.

5 Counties in the Kansas City Metro

Jackson County, MO

Jackson County is the Missouri-side heart of Kansas City. It is the most populous county in the KC metro on the Missouri side and home to downtown Kansas City.

District: Western District of Missouri (Kansas City Division)
Courthouse: 400 E 9th St, Kansas City, MO 64106
Jackson County, MO bankruptcy guide →

Clay County, MO

Clay County is directly north of the Missouri River in the Kansas City metro, including Liberty, Gladstone, and North Kansas City. Cases are filed in the Western District of Missouri.

District: Western District of Missouri (Kansas City Division)
Courthouse: 400 E 9th St, Kansas City, MO 64106
Clay County, MO bankruptcy guide →

Platte County, MO

Platte County is northwest of Kansas City and home to KCI Airport and Parkville. Despite being less urban than Jackson County, it is part of the same federal court division.

District: Western District of Missouri (Kansas City Division)
Courthouse: 400 E 9th St, Kansas City, MO 64106
Platte County, MO bankruptcy guide →

Johnson County, KS

Johnson County is the most affluent and populous county on the Kansas side of the KC metro, including Overland Park, Olathe, Lenexa, and Shawnee. It offers dramatically better bankruptcy exemptions than the Missouri side.

District: District of Kansas (Kansas City Division)
Courthouse: 500 State Ave, Kansas City, KS 66101
Johnson County, KS bankruptcy guide →

Wyandotte County, KS

Wyandotte County is home to Kansas City, Kansas (KCK) and the Unified Government. The bankruptcy courthouse is actually located within Wyandotte County, making it the most convenient filing location in the KC metro.

District: District of Kansas (Kansas City Division)
Courthouse: 500 State Ave, Kansas City, KS 66101

Frequently Asked Questions

Which counties are in the Kansas City bankruptcy metro area?

The Kansas City metro area covers 5 counties: Jackson County, MO, Clay County, MO, Platte County, MO, Johnson County, KS, Wyandotte County, KS. Each county falls under a specific federal bankruptcy court district.

Does it matter which county I live in for bankruptcy?

Yes. Your county determines which federal district court handles your case, and potentially which state exemptions apply. Different districts may have different local rules, trustees, and dismissal rates.
