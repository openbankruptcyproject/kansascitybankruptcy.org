When Chapter 7 Is the Better Choice

  • Your income is below the Missouri or Kansas median for your household size, so you pass the means test automatically
  • Most of your debt is unsecured -- credit cards, medical bills, personal loans
  • You do not have significant non-exempt assets that a trustee would liquidate
  • You want a fresh start quickly -- Chapter 7 discharges in about 3-4 months
  • You are not behind on mortgage or car payments and can continue making current payments
  • You do not have co-signers you need to protect from creditor collection

When Chapter 13 Is the Better Choice

  • You are behind on your mortgage and facing foreclosure -- Chapter 13 lets you cure arrears over 3-5 years while keeping your home
  • You are behind on car payments and facing repossession -- Chapter 13 can cure the default
  • Your income is above the means test threshold and you do not qualify for Chapter 7
  • You have non-exempt property you want to keep (equity above exemption limits)
  • You owe priority taxes that cannot be discharged -- Chapter 13 lets you pay them over time
  • You have a co-signer on consumer debts and want the co-debtor stay to protect them
  • You have a wholly unsecured second mortgage you want to strip off
  • You received a Chapter 7 discharge within the last 8 years and cannot file Chapter 7 again yet

Kansas City Dismissal Rates: A Critical Difference

The success rate for each chapter varies dramatically, and Kansas City filers should understand these numbers before choosing.

Chapter 7 Success Rate

Approximately 95% of Chapter 7 cases in both W.D. Mo. and D. Kan. result in a discharge. Most cases that do not result in discharge are voluntarily dismissed by the debtor, not denied by the court.

Chapter 7 cases are resolved quickly. There is no multi-year plan to fail. Once you file and attend the 341 meeting, discharge is nearly automatic unless there is a specific objection.

Chapter 13 Completion Rate

Chapter 13 has a much lower success rate. In the Western District of Missouri, 40.4% of Chapter 13 cases are dismissed before the debtor receives a discharge. The District of Kansas is somewhat better at 31.4%.

Common reasons for Chapter 13 failure include job loss, income changes, plan payments that were too high to sustain, or simply running out of financial stamina over 3-5 years.

These numbers are based on FJC Integrated Database data covering 2008-2024. The quality of your legal representation matters significantly for Chapter 13 outcomes.

Eligibility Requirements

Chapter 7 Eligibility

  • Must pass the means test (income below state median, or pass the expense calculation)
  • Must complete credit counseling within 180 days before filing
  • Cannot have received a Chapter 7 discharge within the last 8 years
  • Cannot have received a Chapter 13 discharge within the last 6 years
  • Cannot have had a case dismissed for abuse within the last 180 days

2026 Missouri and Kansas income limits →

Chapter 13 Eligibility

  • No means test required
  • Must have regular income sufficient to fund a plan
  • Secured debts must be under $2,750,000 and unsecured debts under $2,750,000 (2026 limits)
  • Must complete credit counseling within 180 days before filing
  • Cannot have received a Chapter 13 discharge within the last 2 years
  • Cannot have received a Chapter 7 discharge within the last 4 years
  • Must be current on tax filing obligations

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the main difference between Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 bankruptcy?

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debts in 3-4 months through liquidation of non-exempt assets. Chapter 13 reorganizes your debts into a 3-5 year repayment plan that lets you keep your property. Chapter 7 requires passing a means test; Chapter 13 does not.

Which chapter has a higher success rate in Kansas City?

Chapter 7 has a significantly higher success rate. Approximately 95% of Chapter 7 cases result in discharge. Chapter 13 has a 40.4% dismissal rate in W.D. Mo. and 31.4% in D. Kan. The quality of legal representation matters significantly for Chapter 13 outcomes.

Can I keep my house and car in Chapter 7?

You can keep your house and car if your equity is within exemption limits. Missouri allows $15,000 homestead and $3,000 vehicle exemptions. Kansas has an unlimited homestead exemption and $20,000 vehicle exemption. If you are behind on payments, Chapter 13 may be better because it lets you catch up over time.

How much does each chapter cost in Kansas City?

Chapter 7 has a $338 filing fee and attorney fees of $1,000-$2,000. Chapter 13 has a $313 filing fee and attorney fees of $2,500-$4,000. Both require two credit counseling courses ($25-$50 total). Full cost breakdown.

What debts can Chapter 13 handle that Chapter 7 cannot?

Chapter 13 can catch up on mortgage arrears to prevent foreclosure, cure car loan defaults, pay priority tax debts over time, and protect co-signers from collection. Chapter 13 can also strip off wholly unsecured junior liens. Learn what debts cannot be discharged in either chapter.
